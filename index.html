Case Study 01

Water U Doing?

Staying hydrated is an extremely important aspect to maintain your health. Unfortunately, many people forget to or are unmotivated to drink water throughout the day.

With this in mind, my team and I took the initiative and designed the "Water U Doing?" mobile app to address this. My involvements in this project include: conducting semi-structured interviews, designing low-fidelity interfaces prototypes, user testing, and adding UI animations to the high-fidelity prototype.

Timeline: December 5, 2022 - January 10, 2023 (approx. 1 month)

Role: UX/UI designer

Collaborators: Annie Baker & Zoe Jonas

Overview

The Solution

In order to address this, my team and I designed a mobile app that helps users drink more water through connecting them and creating a community of health conscious people so they can work together and reach their hydration goals.

The Product

Home page introduces challenges while displaying the user's water intake amount for the day and the user's recommended water intake amount.
Add water page allows users to quickly input their water intake with recommended amount or type in their own.
Challenges show both user and their challenger's water intake of the day. It also keeps track of the user's wins and losses.

User Research

UNDERSTANDING PEOPLE

First, my team and I crafted and sent out a survey on social media to ask about their experience with staying hydrated and drinking water. We recieved about 65 responses and found that many people have experienced symptoms of dehydration and believed that they were not properly hydrating throughout the day. In order to learn more, my team and I then created an interview plan with questions that would help us dive deeper into this trend and understand why it was occurring.

We found 8 people to interview about their experience with hydration with the requirements that they were a young adult (between 18-30 years old) who was concerned about their health. Semi-structured interviews revealed that many people are simply not motivated to drink water.

Survey Results

KEY RESEARCH FINDINGS

  1. People are not motivated to drink water, they are not necessarily forgetting to drink water.
  2. People understand that staying hydrated is important for their health and are willing to drink water.
  3. People have trouble remembering how much water they have drank and do not know how much water they need to drink in order to stay hydrated.
    4. 75% of people reported feeling symtpoms of dehydration to the point that it negative affects them.

MEET SHELBY JONES

From our research we created our user persona, Shelby Jones. Shelby prioritizes her health but struggles to consistenly stay hydrated due to lack of motivation.

PROBLEM STATEMENT

How might we help motivate health conscious individuals to consistently stay hydrated and drink enough water throughout the day?

Ideation

FEATURE PRIORITIZATION

From our user research, we then created a feature prioritization matrix based on the 3 goals that we would help our users achieve:

  1. Create an incentive for users to drink water throughout the day;
  2. Help users stay motivated to drink water consistently and maintain a healthy lifestyle;
  3. Make users aware and help track their daily water intake.

Before diving into the design process, my team and I conducted a competitor analysis to learn about the current water hydration tools on the market and identify: what they are doing well or not well, and what features they are lacking.

To summarize, I learned that while these products all help users manage and track their hydration levels and water intake, they fail to motivate the users to drink water and lacking in social engagement.

VISUALIZING CONCEPTS

With our goals in mind, my team and I sketched out our individual visions for the app which included: the onboarding process, water tracking and other features as well as micro-interactions that will help our users find motivation to stay hydrated.
